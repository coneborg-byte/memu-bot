## Executive Summary
As of February 2026, the sentiment surrounding Meta's Llama 4 remains largely critical. Following a rocky debut in early 2025, the model is increasingly viewed as a "big miss" that has struggled to maintain its competitiveness against rivals like DeepSeek, OpenAI, and Google.

## Key Intelligence (The Alpha)
- **Market Standing**: Analysts state that Llama 4 failed to hold its position at the forefront of AI models for long.
- **Performance Disappointment**: Developers report "underwhelming" results, specifically in coding tasks. One notable report mentioned "complete junk output" from the Llama 4 Scout model in specific real-world tests.
- **Benchmark Controversy**: The community remains skeptical due to accusations that Meta manipulated initial benchmark scores to inflate performance perceptions.
- **Licensing Friction**: The "Open Source" marketing label continues to face pushback due to Meta's restrictive licensing terms.
- **Comparison to Competitors**: Critics find the models underwhelming compared to more specialized or high-performance closed-source models.

## Impact on DNS
The Risk Council should take note that Llama 4 Scout may not be the optimal "next-gen" local model for our Digital Nervous System's logic-heavy tasks. Sticking with Llama 3.1/3.3 or transitioning to more specialized models (like DeepSeek) may provide better ROI for the user's RTX 4060 setup.
